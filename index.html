What I do

Four things I sort out for you

Most companies come to me with one of these on fire and end up wanting help with all four. They're connected: solid architecture makes security easier, and a good team keeps both running after I'm gone.

IT infrastructure & architecture

Most asked

I design the target picture for your systems, cloud and data, then clean up what you already have. Fewer overlapping tools, clear ownership, and an architecture that scales with you instead of fighting you. Cloud-ready, integrated, and documented so the next person can pick it up.

Cybersecurity

Security that fits your size, not an enterprise checklist you'll never finish. I find where you're exposed, fix the urgent gaps first, and put sensible controls, access and policies in place. If you're heading toward ISO 27001 or NIS2, I get you ready without the drama.

Teams & processes

Tools don't run themselves. I help you hire the right people, set up how IT actually works day to day, and coach the team so they can run it without me. Clear roles, sane processes, and the habits that stop small problems becoming outages.

A CIO/CISO on call

Sometimes you just need senior judgement in the room: a board deck to sanity-check, a vendor contract to read, a tough architecture or security call to make. I plug in as your fractional CIO or CISO for as much or as little as you need.

Ways to work together

Fractional CIO / CISO A few days a month, ongoing. I own your IT and security direction and stay close to your team.
Project engagement A defined piece of work: an architecture, a security review, a migration. Fixed scope, hands-on to done.
Advisory on call Senior judgement when you need it, for board decks, vendor calls, and the big decisions.

With a free intro call to understand your setup and what's hurting. From there, most companies start with a half-day IT and security health check, which gives you a clear picture and a plan before committing to anything bigger.

Yes. I'm based in Germany and work with companies across Europe, remote by default, on site when a project needs it.

Are you technical, or more of a strategist?

Both. I can set the architecture and security strategy for your board, and I can get into the detail with your engineers. I don't hand you a slide deck and disappear.

We're small. Do we really need this?

If your tech, data or security is starting to slow you down or keep you up at night, yes. The point of a fractional CIO/CISO is that you get senior judgement sized to where you are now, without the cost of a full-time hire.

We agree a simple scope up front, whether that's a one-off review, a fixed-scope project, or a few days a month ongoing. No surprises and no vague hourly billing.
